Skill Games in Pennsylvania: Your Questions Answered

Everything you need to know about skill games, legality, earnings, installation, and getting started in Pennsylvania.

Legal & Compliance

Are skill games legal in Pennsylvania?

Yes. In December 2023, Pennsylvania's Commonwealth Court unanimously ruled that skill games are legal and are not gambling devices because outcomes are determined by player skill rather than chance. The case is currently under review by the Pennsylvania Supreme Court, but skill games remain legal and operational throughout the state.

What is the difference between skill games and slot machines?

Skill games require player interaction and decision-making that directly affects the outcome. Players must demonstrate skill to win. Slot machines, by contrast, produce purely random results. This legal distinction is why Pennsylvania's Commonwealth Court ruled skill games are not gambling devices.

Do I need a special license to operate skill games?

No. Unlike slot machines or video gaming terminals, skill games do not currently require a state gaming license in Pennsylvania. Your existing business license is sufficient. Skill games can be placed in bars, restaurants, convenience stores, game rooms, laundromats, and other establishments. Note that legislation is being considered that may introduce a licensing framework in the future.

What is the Pennsylvania Supreme Court skill games case?

The Pennsylvania Supreme Court heard oral arguments in November 2025 on whether skill games should be classified as gambling devices. The Commonwealth Court previously ruled unanimously that they are legal. A ruling is expected in 2026. Skill games remain fully legal and operational while the case is pending.

A revenue share agreement means we place and maintain the machines at your location for free. Revenue generated by the machines is split between you and us on a weekly basis. Typical splits give the location 35-50% of net revenue depending on volume and location type. There are no long lock-in contracts.
