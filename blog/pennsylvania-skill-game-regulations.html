Pennsylvania Skill Game Regulations: What Operators Need to Know

Updated January 2025 | 9 min read

Understanding the regulatory landscape is essential for any Pennsylvania skill game operator. Here's what you need to know about current regulations, proposed legislation, and what the future may hold.

Current Legal Status

As of January 2025, skill games are legal in Pennsylvania following the December 2023 Commonwealth Court ruling. The court found that skill-based games are not gambling devices because player skill—not chance—determines outcomes.

Key Points from the Ruling

  • Skill games are NOT slot machines under Pennsylvania law
  • No gaming license required to operate skill games
  • Skill games can operate in any business that permits gaming entertainment
  • The ruling applies statewide

Supreme Court Review

The Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board appealed the Commonwealth Court decision to the state Supreme Court. Key developments:

  • November 2024: Oral arguments heard
  • Status: Decision pending
  • Current operation: Skill games remain legal while the case is reviewed

Proposed Legislation

Several bills have been introduced in the Pennsylvania legislature to regulate and tax skill games. Most proposals share common elements:

Typical Proposed Framework

  • Licensing: Operators and locations would need licenses
  • Taxation: Proposed rates range from 16% to 42% of gross revenue
  • Machine limits: Caps on machines per location (often 5-10)
  • Location restrictions: Distance requirements from schools, churches
  • Age verification: 21+ requirements with ID checks

What Regulation Could Mean for Operators

If regulation passes, operators would likely see:

  • New licensing requirements and fees
  • Revenue taxation (reducing net income)
  • Compliance requirements and reporting
  • Potentially, legitimization and long-term stability

Current Operating Requirements

While skill games don't require special gaming licenses, operators should ensure:

Business Requirements

  • Valid business license for your location
  • Appropriate zoning for your business type
  • Compliance with local ordinances
  • Proper insurance coverage

Machine Requirements

  • Machines must be genuine skill-based games (not chance-based)
  • Games should have verifiable skill elements
  • Work with reputable distributors who provide compliant machines

Age Restrictions

  • Players should be 18+ (21+ in establishments serving alcohol)
  • Post age restriction signage
  • Monitor for underage play

Municipal Variations

Some Pennsylvania municipalities have enacted local ordinances regarding skill games. Before installing machines, verify:

  • Your municipality hasn't banned skill games
  • Any local licensing requirements
  • Zoning restrictions for gaming devices
  • Hours of operation requirements

Industry Advocacy

Several industry groups advocate for reasonable skill game regulation:

  • Pennsylvania Skill Games Coalition
  • Small business associations
  • Hospitality industry groups

These groups generally support fair taxation and regulation that allows continued operation while addressing state concerns.

What to Watch For

Key developments operators should monitor:

  1. Supreme Court decision: Expected to affirm or reverse the Commonwealth Court ruling
  2. Legislative action: New bills in the PA House and Senate
  3. Budget negotiations: Skill game taxes often discussed as revenue sources
  4. Local ordinances: Municipal actions in your area

Staying Compliant

To operate responsibly and prepare for potential regulation:

  1. Work only with reputable, established distributors
  2. Ensure machines are genuine skill-based games
  3. Maintain proper business documentation
  4. Keep accurate revenue records
  5. Stay informed about legislative developments
  6. Join industry associations for updates
