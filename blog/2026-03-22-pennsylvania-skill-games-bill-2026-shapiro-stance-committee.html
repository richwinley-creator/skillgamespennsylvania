Pennsylvania Skill Games Bill 2026: Shapiro Administration's Latest Stance & Committee Progress

March 22, 2026 By Skill Games Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania Skill Games Bill 2026: Where Does the Shapiro Administration Stand?

As we head deeper into 2026, Pennsylvania operators and advocates are watching closely as skill games legislation continues its journey through the state capitol. The question on everyone's mind: where does Governor Josh Shapiro's administration actually stand on legalizing and regulating Pennsylvania skill games?

The answer has proven more nuanced than many expected, and understanding the current landscape is essential for anyone invested in the future of skill games in Pennsylvania.

The Shapiro Administration's Evolving Position

When Shapiro took office in 2023, his stance on skill games was cautious but not dismissive. Unlike some governors who have outright opposed skill games expansion, Shapiro indicated openness to discussion—provided the right regulatory framework and revenue structure were in place.

By early 2026, that position has crystallized somewhat. The administration appears willing to support Pennsylvania skill games legislation that includes:

Revenue-Positive Structure: Any skill games bill must generate meaningful tax revenue for the state. The administration's budget pressures mean that skill games are being evaluated primarily as a revenue source, not just a gaming expansion.

Local Control Measures: The administration has emphasized that municipalities should retain authority to opt-in or opt-out of skill games operations. This protects small communities from gaming expansion they don't want while allowing jurisdictions to benefit economically.

Licensing & Regulatory Framework: Shapiro's team has made clear that robust licensing requirements, background checks, and ongoing compliance monitoring are non-negotiable. This positions skill games as a heavily regulated industry, similar to gaming operations.

Committee Progress: What's Actually Happening in Harrisburg

At the legislative level, Pennsylvania skill games have moved beyond theoretical discussion. Multiple committees have engaged with skill games bills, though progress has been uneven.

The House Gaming Oversight Committee has held hearings examining skill games regulation and revenue potential. These sessions have brought together operators, municipalities, consumer advocates, and gaming experts to evaluate the practical and financial implications of legalization.

What's notable: these hearings have been relatively balanced. Rather than dismissing skill games outright, committees are seriously examining operational models, tax structures, and regulatory requirements. This represents meaningful progress compared to 2023 and 2024, when skill games were often treated as a fringe issue.

The Senate has been slightly slower to engage, but key appropriations committee members have signaled interest in how skill games revenue could address Pennsylvania's structural budget challenges. With state revenues under pressure, the economic argument for skill games is gaining traction in ways it hadn't before.

Why 2026 Is Different for PA Skill Games Legislation

Several factors have shifted the conversation in favor of skill games legalization in Pennsylvania:

Virginia's Success: The passage of SB 661 in Virginia in 2024 and its smooth 2025 implementation has provided a working model. Pennsylvania legislators can now point to a neighboring state that successfully legalized skill games without major problems, removing some of the fear of the unknown.

Local Government Buy-In: Pennsylvania municipalities that have lost tax revenue due to economic shifts are now more interested in skill games as an alternative revenue stream. This grassroots support from local leaders is changing the calculus for state legislators.

Budget Pressure: Pennsylvania's structural budget deficit has made any potential revenue source more attractive. Skill games—which could generate $50-100+ million annually depending on the regulatory model—are being taken seriously in budget discussions.

Operator Sophistication: Unlike the early 2020s, Pennsylvania skill games operators today include established gaming companies with experience in other states. This professionalism has made the industry case more credible to policymakers.

What Obstacles Remain

Despite progress, Pennsylvania skill games still face headwinds:

Casino Industry Resistance: Pennsylvania's existing casino industry remains concerned about skill games cannibalizing their customer base. This opposition carries weight in Harrisburg, where established gaming interests have significant lobbying resources.

Anti-Gambling Coalition: Social conservative and problem gambling advocates continue to oppose expansion, though their influence has modestly declined as economic arguments have become more prominent.

Regulatory Complexity: Designing a workable regulatory framework that satisfies consumer protection, local control, and revenue concerns is genuinely complicated. No quick consensus has emerged on key details.

Timeline and Next Steps for PA Skill Games

Based on current committee activity and administration signals, here's what to expect in 2026:

Spring 2026: Additional hearings and bill introductions in both chambers. Expect companion bills in House and Senate.

Summer 2026: Committee markup sessions where bills are refined. This is where detailed regulatory framework gets decided.

Fall 2026: Possible floor votes if bills gain sufficient committee support. Election-year dynamics may accelerate or delay depending on political calculations.

What Skill Games Advocates Should Do Now

For those who believe Pennsylvania skill games legalization would benefit the state, this is the time to engage:

Share operator success stories from other states with your legislators. Document how skill games have generated revenue without creating social problems in Virginia, North Carolina, and other jurisdictions. Encourage your local municipality to formally signal interest in skill games revenue to their state representatives. Attend legislative hearings and provide public testimony about why Pennsylvania skill games make policy sense. Connect with responsible operators who can articulate the industry's commitment to compliance and player protection.

Conclusion: Pennsylvania's Skill Games Moment

Pennsylvania skill games legislation is closer to reality in March 2026 than ever before. The Shapiro administration's openness, combined with committee activity and budget pressure, has created genuine momentum. While obstacles remain, the conversation has shifted from whether Pennsylvania should legalize skill games to how to do so responsibly.

For advocates, operators, and interested Pennsylvanians, this is the moment to stay engaged and push for legalization that benefits both the state's finances and Pennsylvania's gaming ecosystem.
