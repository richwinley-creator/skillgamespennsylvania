Skill Games for Bars in Pennsylvania: Complete Guide

Updated January 2025 | 8 min read

Bars consistently outperform every other location type for skill games. Pennsylvania bar owners are earning $3,000-$6,000+ monthly with the right setup. Here's how to maximize your revenue.

Why Bars Are #1 for Skill Games

Several factors make bars ideal for skill games:

  • Late hours: Evening and late-night are peak playing times
  • Captive audience: Patrons are relaxed and looking for entertainment
  • Social atmosphere: Gaming becomes part of the bar experience
  • Adult environment: No concerns about underage players
  • Alcohol service: Creates a relaxed entertainment mindset

Revenue Expectations for Bars

Bar Type Monthly Revenue (Your Share)

Optimal Machine Placement

Best Locations Within Your Bar

  • Near the bar: Players can easily get drinks while playing
  • Back room/game area: Dedicated gaming space works well
  • Visible from entrance: Draws attention from arriving patrons
  • Away from TVs: Reduces competition for attention during games

Placement Tips

  • Ensure good lighting—not too dim
  • Provide comfortable seating
  • Keep area clean and maintained
  • Consider sightlines for staff

How Many Machines?

Bar Size Recommended Machines
Small neighborhood bar 2-3 machines
Medium bar/tavern 3-4 machines
Large bar/sports bar 4-6 machines
Bar with game room 6-10+ machines

Maximizing Your Bar's Revenue

1. Promote the Games

  • Mention skill games to regular customers
  • Post signage about the games
  • Train staff to direct interested patrons

2. Create the Right Atmosphere

  • Good lighting in the gaming area
  • Comfortable seating at machines
  • Easy drink access for players

3. Extended Hours

Bars that stay open later typically see higher skill game revenue. The 10 PM - 2 AM window is often the most profitable.

4. Keep Machines Running

Report any issues immediately. A down machine is lost revenue. We provide fast service to minimize downtime.

Impact on Food and Beverage Sales

Skill games typically help bar sales:

  • Longer stays: Players spend more time (and money) at your bar
  • New customers: Some people visit specifically for the games
  • Increased drink sales: Players buy drinks while playing
  • Food purchases: Extended stays mean more food orders

What About Pool Tables and Other Games?

Skill games complement rather than replace traditional bar games:

  • Different appeal than pool, darts, or video games
  • Attract a different player base
  • Many bars successfully offer both
  • Skill games require less space than a pool table

Common Concerns Addressed

"Will this attract the wrong crowd?"

Skill game players are typically working adults looking for entertainment—the same demographic already visiting bars. Many become regular customers.

"Do I need any special license?"

No gaming license required. Your existing liquor license and business permits are sufficient.

"What if my landlord objects?"

Most commercial leases allow skill games. We recommend checking your lease or discussing with your landlord.

"How much space do I need?"

Each machine needs about 10-15 square feet including player space. We work with your existing layout.
