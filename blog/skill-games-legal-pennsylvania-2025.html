Are Skill Games Legal in Pennsylvania? 2025 Update

Updated January 2025 | 8 min read

If you're considering adding skill games to your Pennsylvania business, the first question on your mind is likely about legality. The good news: skill games are currently legal in Pennsylvania following a landmark court ruling. Here's everything you need to know.

The Short Answer

Yes, skill games are legal in Pennsylvania. In December 2023, Pennsylvania's Commonwealth Court issued a unanimous ruling declaring that skill-based games are not gambling devices because the outcome depends primarily on player skill rather than chance.

The 2023 Commonwealth Court Ruling

The case centered on whether skill games qualify as "slot machines" under Pennsylvania's gambling laws. The Commonwealth Court found that they do not, for several key reasons:

  • Skill determines outcomes: Unlike slot machines where random number generators determine results, skill games require players to make decisions that directly affect whether they win or lose.
  • Consistent player performance: Expert players can achieve significantly better results than beginners, demonstrating that skill—not luck—is the predominant factor.
  • No random prize distribution: Payouts are tied to player performance, not random chance.

Current Status: Pennsylvania Supreme Court Review

The Pennsylvania Gaming Control Board appealed the Commonwealth Court's decision to the state Supreme Court. Oral arguments were heard in November 2024, and a decision is pending.

What this means for operators: While the Supreme Court reviews the case, skill games remain legal and continue to operate throughout Pennsylvania. There are currently approximately 80,000 skill game machines operating in the state.

Why This Matters for Your Business

The legal status of skill games creates an opportunity for Pennsylvania business owners:

  • Bars and restaurants can add a significant revenue stream
  • Convenience stores can attract customers and increase dwell time
  • Game rooms can operate legally with skill-based machines

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a special license to have skill games?

No. Unlike slot machines, which require a state gaming license, skill games can be placed in any business that allows gaming entertainment. Standard business licenses apply.

Can skill games be in any location?

Skill games are commonly placed in bars, restaurants, convenience stores, gas stations, game rooms, social clubs (VFWs, American Legions), and laundromats. Location-specific regulations may vary by municipality.

What happens if the Supreme Court rules against skill games?

If the Supreme Court reverses the Commonwealth Court ruling, there would likely be a transition period. However, many legal experts expect the ruling to be upheld given the clear distinction between skill-based and chance-based gaming.

Are there any proposed regulations?

Several bills have been introduced in the Pennsylvania legislature proposing to regulate and tax skill games. These proposals generally acknowledge the games' legality while seeking to create a regulatory framework and tax structure.
