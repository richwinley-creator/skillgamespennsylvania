16% vs. 52%: The PA Skill Games Tax Battle That Operators Can't Afford to Ignore

Pennsylvania skill games operators are caught in a three-way squeeze: a Senate bill proposing a 16% tax rate, a governor's budget demanding 52%, and a state Supreme Court ruling that could upend both before the ink dries. With the June 30 budget deadline approaching, the window for operators to understand — and prepare for — what's coming is narrowing fast.

16% Senate Bill 626 proposed tax rate for operators
52% Governor Shapiro's proposed tax rate (matching VGTs)
June 30 Pennsylvania's constitutional budget deadline

The Two Bills on the Table

There is no shortage of proposals for how Pennsylvania should handle skill games. But two are driving the real debate heading into the 2026 budget cycle.

Senate Bill 626, sponsored by Sen. Gene Yaw, proposes a 16% tax on operator gross revenue. It's framed as an operator-friendly structure that keeps skill games viable for the small businesses, VFWs, fire companies, and convenience stores that rely on them. Under SB 626:

  • Operators pay a $25,000 initial license fee and $5,000 annual renewal
  • Host locations — liquor-license holders, tobacco/lottery retailers, nonprofits — pay a $250 annual hosting fee
  • Hosts are limited to a maximum of 5 terminals per location, with no more than 2 machines per 500 square feet
  • Net profits are split 40% to the host, 40% to the operator, 20% to the distributor

Sen. Yaw's argument is straightforward: a lower tax rate keeps the market intact and generates more durable revenue over time. "I choose to support the economic backbone of Pennsylvania — our small businesses — over the corporate conglomerates," Yaw has said.

Governor Shapiro's budget proposal goes a different direction. His $53.3 billion spending plan for 2026-27 projects nearly $766 million in skill games revenue — but only if machines are taxed at 52%, the same rate applied to video gaming terminals (VGTs) found at licensed truck stops.

At a March 11 budget hearing, Revenue Secretary Pat Browne defended the 52% rate as "consistent and fair tax policy." The logic: skill games and VGTs operate similarly, so they should face equal tax treatment. Critics point out the comparison ignores the radically different business contexts — a VGT at a major truck stop is not the same as two machines in a rural American Legion post.

"Skill games are a part of the revenue of a lot of businesses. Small family businesses. Fire companies. VFW's. Restaurants. American Legions." — Rep. Jamie Barton, PA House Appropriations Committee

The Third Variable: The Supreme Court

Both proposals assume skill games remain legal and operational. That's not guaranteed.

The Pennsylvania Supreme Court heard arguments in the skill games case in late November 2025. The central question: are skill games legally distinguishable from slot machines under Pennsylvania law, or are they effectively unregulated gambling devices that should have been banned years ago?

Lower courts have ruled in favor of skill games, finding that the skill component meaningfully distinguishes them from slot machines. But several Supreme Court justices appeared skeptical at oral argument. One asked whether it was legal to give people the "opportunity to gamble outside the gambling statutes" — not a softball question.

The ruling is expected sometime in 2026. It could arrive before the June 30 budget deadline, after it, or right in the middle of a legislative negotiation — a timeline that makes planning difficult for operators and lawmakers alike.

If the Supreme Court rules skill games are illegal, legislative proposals become moot immediately. If it upholds their legality, it removes the court wildcard and pushes the entire fight back to Harrisburg — where the 16% vs. 52% debate will intensify with new urgency.

What Operators Should Do Right Now

The honest answer is that no one knows what the final structure will look like. But operators who prepare now will be in a far better position regardless of which outcome materializes.

  • Understand your numbers at both tax rates. Run your revenue projections under a 16% scenario and a 52% scenario. Know at what point your machines become unprofitable at a given location. This is baseline intelligence you need before any policy is finalized.
  • Know your host locations. Any regulatory framework will require formal agreements between operators and hosts. If your current hosting arrangements are informal, start getting them documented. This will matter when licensing requirements arrive.
  • Track the Supreme Court docket. The ruling will likely come without much warning. Monitor the PA Supreme Court's docket for the Pace-O-Matic case and be ready to respond quickly — because Harrisburg will move fast once the ruling drops.
  • Engage your associations. The Pennsylvania Petroleum Association, the Pennsylvania Restaurant and Lodging Association, and skill game trade groups are all active in Harrisburg on this issue. Operators who are plugged in will get earlier warning on legislative movement.
  • Budget for the higher rate as a downside case. If you can make your operations work at 52%, you'll be positioned to survive the worst outcome and thrive if the final rate comes in lower.

The Budget Deadline Is the Real Forcing Function

Pennsylvania's constitution requires a balanced budget by June 30. Governor Shapiro's proposal relies on $766 million in skill games revenue to help close a gap he's covering by drawing down the entire Rainy Day Fund. That creates significant political pressure to resolve the skill games question — one way or another — before summer.

If the Supreme Court strikes down skill games before June 30, Harrisburg loses that $766 million line and faces a painful scramble. If the court upholds legality, lawmakers will face intense pressure to pass something — either the 16% Senate framework or a compromise — before the fiscal year ends.

Either way, the next 15 weeks will likely determine the regulatory future of skill games in Pennsylvania for years to come. Operators who are watching, prepared, and connected will be in the best position to respond.
