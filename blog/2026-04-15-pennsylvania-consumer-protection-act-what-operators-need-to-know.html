Pennsylvania Skill Game Consumer Protection Act: What Operators Need to Know

April 15, 2026 6 min read

Pennsylvania Representative Mark Waxman has introduced the Skill Game Consumer Protection Act—a new bill designed to add consumer safeguards to Pennsylvania's skill games market. Here's what operators need to know about the proposed regulations and what this means for your business.

What Is the Skill Game Consumer Protection Act?

Introduced in April 2026, the Skill Game Consumer Protection Act represents a shift in Pennsylvania's regulatory approach. Rather than simply taxing skill games revenue (as proposed in Governor Shapiro's earlier framework), this bill focuses on consumer protections while establishing clearer operational standards for skill games venues and operators.

The bill follows national trends toward consumer-focused regulations in the gaming industry, particularly around problem gambling protections, responsible gaming disclosures, and operational transparency.

Key Proposed Regulations

While the full text of the bill is still being circulated, early details suggest the Consumer Protection Act addresses:

  • Problem Gambling Protections: Mandatory signage and resources for players about responsible gaming
  • Age Verification: Enhanced verification requirements to ensure machines are not accessed by underage players
  • Operational Transparency: Operators must disclose game odds, payout percentages, and house edge to consumers
  • Venue Compliance Standards: Licensing requirements for venues hosting skill games, with background checks for operators
  • Data Reporting: Regular reporting of player activity and revenue to state gaming authorities for oversight

How Does This Differ from Governor Shapiro's Tax Proposal?

Governor Shapiro's earlier framework focused on revenue generation—a 52% tax rate to fund education and state programs. The Consumer Protection Act takes a different approach: it prioritizes consumer safeguards and operational standards without specifying a particular tax rate.

This dual-track approach suggests Pennsylvania may be moving toward a hybrid regulatory model—both consumer protections and state revenue capture. If both bills advance, operators would face both compliance requirements AND significant tax obligations.

What This Means for Operators

If the Consumer Protection Act passes, Pennsylvania operators should prepare for:

  1. Increased Compliance Costs: Enhanced signage, age verification systems, and reporting infrastructure will require investment.
  2. Operational Transparency Requirements: You'll need to disclose game odds and payout information—this affects your competitive positioning and player expectations.
  3. Licensing and Background Checks: Operators will need state approval, adding time and cost to market entry and venue placement.
  4. Ongoing Reporting Obligations: Regular data submission to state authorities creates administrative overhead.
  5. Responsible Gaming Training: You and your venue partners will likely need to complete training and certification on responsible gaming practices.

Is This Good or Bad for Pennsylvania Skill Games?

Consumer protections are a double-edged sword for operators:

Positive: Clear regulations provide legal certainty. Once the rules are formalized, operators can invest confidently in Pennsylvania without fear of sudden enforcement crackdowns or market closures. Regulated markets are sustainable markets.

Negative: Compliance costs reduce profit margins. Combined with the proposed 52% tax rate, operators will face significant financial pressure. The key question: Will the Pennsylvania market be large enough to support profitable operations under both consumer protections AND high taxation?

Timeline and Next Steps

The Consumer Protection Act is in its early stages. Here's what to expect:

  • Q2 2026: Bill gains support or faces opposition in legislative committees
  • Q3-Q4 2026: House and Senate debate and amendments
  • 2027: Potential passage and implementation timeline set

Pennsylvania's legislative calendar moves slowly. Even if the bill advances this year, implementation could be 12-18 months away. This gives operators time to prepare, but not much.

What Should Operators Do Now?

Start preparing:

  • Monitor the Bill: Check the Pennsylvania Legislature website for updates on the Consumer Protection Act and track its progress.
  • Assess Your Compliance Readiness: Review current venues and machines against the likely requirements. What upgrades will you need?
  • Build Relationships: Start conversations with venues about licensing requirements and shared compliance costs.
  • Plan for Hybrid Taxation: Assume both the Consumer Protection Act regulations AND the 52% tax rate could pass. Model profitability under that scenario.
  • Engage in Advocacy: Consider joining industry associations pushing back on unreasonably high tax rates or compliance costs.

The Bottom Line

Pennsylvania's Consumer Protection Act signals that skill games regulation is moving from "if" to "when." The market is shifting toward formalized, regulated operations. Operators who embrace compliance early and build strong venue relationships will be best positioned to succeed in Pennsylvania's regulated skill games market.
